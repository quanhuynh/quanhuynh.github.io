This was my first personal project in web development (besides building my own website). I coded LIP from scratch in HTML and CSS, with a bit of JavaScript and jQuery.

LIP is a sample home page of a mock news site. I got inspiration from my own experience in navigating news websites. In my opinion, many don't fully prioritize the flow of information on a page.

Sites like CNN and Fox focus on putting as much information on the front page as possible without providing a "map" for the reader to follow.

In my implementation of LIP, features I focused on include:

  • A clean top bar for immediate access to easy navigation.
  • A slideshow of featured articles to capture the reader's attention.
  • More featured articles in the Latest News section.
  • And on the main content of the front page: card-style boxes with popular tags so the reader can stay informed without being distracted by a list of text-only articles

While LIP may be less dense in terms of information than some news sites out there, I imagine that it would be more appealing than columns and rows of text.
